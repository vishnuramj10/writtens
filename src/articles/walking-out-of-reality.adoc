Zzzz..zzzzzz. Felt a kick in? Felt like you are falling down? That happens so many times when we sleep. That's the brain doing some trick on us while we shut down. That's where dreams start.

The scientific part of dreams is that they mostly occur during our REM (Rapid Eye Movement) sleep cycle, where our memories become vivid and play out as scenes.

You do see characters from your real life play out as a movie. Sometimes they are about a particular thing you are curious or tense about in real life, where it plays out, and sometimes it's very random. Sometimes it's with people you hardly care about who come into your dreams. I am not here to talk about why, but more to talk about what it could mean.

My favorite theory, and the most important one which I believe is true, is that many dreams happen in reality using the theory of a multi-dimensional parallel universe. Can you imagine millions of universes sharing the same characters but with different lifestyles playing out? Could it be a branch in timelines where each action of yours is taking you to a different universe, and that's what gets into your subconscious because both versions of you are sharing a quantum image of the same space and it's wrapping itself into your subconscious?

They are choices of our parallel lives, and the multiversal theory is something which many scientists have long been fascinated with. Many have reported dreams of flying, so unlike how we are bound by the laws of space and time, there might be a version of reality which has gravitational laws of another planet. When we experience déjà vu, a French term meaning "already dreamt," is a sense of familiarity, it might be a fleeting connection to a parallel version of ourselves who has lived a similar moment in a different universe.

There are multiple theories on the effect of our dreams in parallel to the quantum world. Some interpretations of quantum physics suggest that every possible outcome occurs, however we only experience one outcome while the remaining outcomes occur in a different world. This directly pushes into the concept of branching, where each alternate choice you make exists in a quantum world of parallel choice, and therefore your dreams are a reality in that particular branch split. They are nothing but silent quantum echoes, and it is indeed called a quantum consciousness theory.

Although much of it seems overambitious for it to happen, most scientists do believe dreams are a byproduct of memory consolidation. It is how our brain processes ongoing things, people we meet, new experiences, and our regular thoughts into action. There are cases where you think about something too much and it does happen in your dream. It's how our brain is processing and connecting our previous and current days of life.

Our sleep has four cycles, out of which one is the REM cycle. Dreams themselves usually last between a few seconds and 30 minutes in length. There is an entire movie on this concept, and it was made by none other than Christopher Nolan. The movie Inception explains and theorizes its own concept of dreams and how they can be used to manipulate others' minds.

Well, other famous thinkers like Sigmund Freud believed dreams were often a form of wish fulfillment and that a subject could act out desires he or she could not fulfill in waking life. Well, not to argue, but I have read many psychological theories: one where dreams are direct mental expressions and have a disguised meaning to real life; another where dreams had no intrinsic meaning and were just a side effect of the brain's normal activity while it tries to make sense of electrical signals; and another where dreaming is a form of threat awareness and rehearsal for similar events.

While there are so many theories on dreams, every time I dream, I do feel it is something which my inner self wants, or something which my subconscious thinks about, something which I think about daily and it happens. While many dreams we might not remember and forget, many dreams are random and feel so real, while many dreams speak as if they did happen in an alternate reality. It is left to us on how we theorize it happens, but I do hope our dreams are a mixture of all theories and certainly that they are hopping out of our understanding of reality and the universe.
